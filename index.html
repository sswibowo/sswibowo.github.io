About me

I am a lecturer in finance at the Department of Management, Faculty of Economics and Business, Universitas Indonesia. I started my academic career by joining this Department in January 2004 as a teaching assistant/tutor and promoted as teaching staff in 2007. My research interests lie in financial economics, mainly household finance and risk management. Check my publications here. Currently, I am based in Edinburgh, UK and also affliated with Queen Margaret University, Edinburgh.

I hold a bachelor's degree in economics (finance) and a master's degree in risk management from Universitas Indonesia. I obtained my doctoral degree from Durham University, the United Kingdom in 2015.

I was appointed as the head of the academic partnership of Directorate for Collaborative Affairs from 2015 to 2019, where I was responsible for overseeing academic cooperation at the university level. I was deputy editor of the Indonesian Capital Market Review from 2015 to 2019.

Codes for research

LaTeX for academic writing

I modified UI thesis style written by Andreas Febrian et al. using LaTeX typesetting system. Since this is not an official template by the University, it (hopefully) follows and complies with Universitas Indonesia’s thesis writing rules. The updated codes can be download here. The template is in Bahasa Indonesia. Any suggestions are welcome.

LaTeX can be obtained from MacTeX (for Mac users) and MiKTeX website (for Windows users). For Mac users, I recommend to use TeXShop as LaTeX editor and for Windows users, I suggest to use Texmaker or TeXnicCenter respectively. Any tables in Excel can be converted into LaTeX codes by using excel2latex add-ins. I recommend Jabref, Mendeley, or Zotero for bibliography reference management software.

Richard Stanton of UCLA Berkeley provides a very useful document in writing finance papers using LaTeX on his website. Some publishers also provide LaTeX template, for example INFORMS, Springer, and Elsevier. For more templates, visit latextemplates.com.

R for finance

For those who are interested to use opensource software for finance research, CRAN repository contains more than 3000 packages that provide various additional capabilities. R can be used in a programming language (like Python) or in a script environment for data analysis.
